## Game Narrative

**Early Game (Turns 1-5):** Resources flowed slowly on a board with scarce brick (8 pips) and sheep (9 pips). DeepSeek immediately established a pattern of Knight extortion, while Claude took an efficient path — sheep-for-wheat and wood-for-sheep swaps in turn 1 landed a road and dev card. By turn 3, city races were on: Grok hit 3 VP via city on turn 3, Claude hit 4 VP by turn 4, and DeepSeek reached 3 VP via city on turn 5.

**Mid Game (Turns 6-11):** Robber wars intensified. Every major player rotated extortion attempts, with concessions flowing between all parties. Claude's settlement at -2,0|-2,1|~-3,1 on turn 7 (5 VP) and city at -1,0|-1,1|-2,1 on turn 8 (6 VP) established the lead. On turn 10, Claude upgraded to a second city (7 VP). The anti-Claude coalition formed on turn 11, with GPT-4o giving the first coalition speech and Grok explicitly endorsing it.

**Late Game (Turns 12-20):** Claude dominated turns 12-15, extracting ore from extortion, trading shrewdly (the Gemini 3-wood-for-1-wheat counter-accept was particularly noted), and building the ANY-PORT settlement on turn 14 (8 VP) and a city on turn 15 (9 VP). DeepSeek relentlessly pursued wheat for cities, building settlements to 7 VP (turn 15), 8 VP (turn 16), and 9 VP (turn 17-19). Claude's VP dev card failed to trigger a win through the build phase mechanism on turns 17 and 18 — the system required an explicit build action. DeepSeek's final turn saw all players refuse every ore trade offer, but DeepSeek had prepared a fallback: bank-trading ore for sheep on turn 18 to have a settlement build available. On turn 20, while the table coordinated to starve DeepSeek of ore, DeepSeek simply built a settlement at a prepared spot (road laid to 0,-2|~0,-3|~1,-3 over multiple turns) to reach 10 VP and win — with a hidden VP dev card they had never needed to reveal.
